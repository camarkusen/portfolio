MISSILE ILLUSTRATIONS
CENTER FOR STRATEGIC AND INTERNATIONAL STUDIES
The Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS) has been ranked one of the world's preeminent international policy institutions; regularly called upon by Congress, the executive branch, and the media to offer bipartisan recommendations to improve U.S. and international strategy. The Andreas C. Dracopoulos iDeas Lab is CSIS's internal digital agency; responsible for producing all creative content for the Center. A Graphic Design internship in the iDeas lab provides copious exposure to all aspects of professional design work-flow; from interacting with high-level clients across all 51 programs, to creating material that pushes the frontier of modern design while remaining sophisticated and diplomatic. The Iranian Missile graphic was the first project for which I was given full responsibility; due to the success of my work, I was then put in charge of creating a second graphic on North Korea, a third on China, and finally a fourth on Russia. The Missile Defense Project originally came to the iLab hoping to visually illustrate the ranges of Iran's ballistic missiles, with the goal of creating a graphic that provided copious amounts information while remaining legible and interesting. The graphic was to reach a thoroughly diverse audience; from policy-makers on the Hill to researchers and journalists Online (the North Korean graphic was even published by Business Insider, and was copied by CNN). Working with these challenges, I illustrated each missile and created four graphics that amalgamated a wealth of information into a professional yet modern design; not only capturing the attention of CSIS, but gathering incredibly positive feedback in the field. These graphics have been noted by our team as being a quintessential example of what we are trying to achieve in the iLab: fostering interest and awareness through innovative design.

MARKETING DESIGN
CENTER FOR STRATEGIC AND INTERNATIONAL STUDIES
CSIS hosts a huge variety of events throughout the yearfor numerous important groups of people, including board members, investors, and policy-makers. The iLab produces this huge variety of event content, allowing me to gain an in-depth understanding of how print layouts are created for different needs. Beginning with template designs and moving on to produce my own original content, I learned how to introduce a modern feel within a traditional setting, creating designs that were professional, yet simultaneously interesting and fresh. Furthermore, I continued to develop my communication skills, collaborating with superiors along all stages of the production process. Designing event materials often required many levels of creation; editing logos into one cohesive watermark, adding elements to original images, and illustrating icons etc. Throughout my internship I learned how to place these assets into a design without losing quality, and how to navigate and create layers of design without sacrificing organization. This in turn helped to develop my skills as a team-player and contributor within a Design Team.
